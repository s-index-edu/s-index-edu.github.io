S-Index Graph

More about how S-Index is calculated:

  • For a conference, we calculate the number of papers each author has published in that conference since its inception. The S-Index is the largest number k such that at least k authors have each published at least k papers in the conference.

  • Next, we define the "S-Index set" of a conference with an S-Index of k as the set of authors with at least k publications in that conference. The size of the S-Index set can exceed k.

  • Additionally, we define the "S-i-Index" of a conference as the number of authors who have published at least i papers in that conference.
